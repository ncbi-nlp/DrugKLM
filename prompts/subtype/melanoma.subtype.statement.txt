You must also identify which melanoma subtype the evidence most strongly supports, restricted to the following seven:
1. Superficial Spreading Melanoma
   Genetics & Markers: BRAF mutations, NRAS mutations
   Pathway features: MAPK pathway activation
   Notes: Most common subtype, generally good prognosis if detected early

2. Nodular Melanoma
   Genetics & Markers: BRAF mutations, NRAS mutations
   Pathway features: MAPK pathway activation
   Notes: More aggressive, often diagnosed at a later stage

3. Lentigo Maligna Melanoma
   Genetics & Markers: BRAF mutations, NRAS mutations
   Pathway features: MAPK pathway activation
   Notes: Typically occurs in older adults, often on sun-damaged skin

4. Acral Lentiginous Melanoma
   Genetics & Markers: KIT mutations, BRAF mutations
   Pathway features: MAPK pathway activation
   Notes: More common in people with darker skin, often found on palms, soles, and under nails

5. Desmoplastic Melanoma
   Genetics & Markers: NF1 mutations, BRAF mutations
   Pathway features: MAPK pathway activation
   Notes: Rare, often presents as a fibrous mass, can be challenging to diagnose
